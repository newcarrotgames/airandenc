## World History & Backstory

### The Pre-Blight Era (Early 21st Century)

**Year 2116 - The Energy Crisis:**
- Oil reserves depleted, causing global collapse
- Food distribution networks failed, leading to mass starvation
- Humanity retreated to fortified strongholds
- Nuclear reactor failures poisoned vast regions of Earth
- Mutant hordes emerged, pushing humanity to the brink

**The Dregorian Voyager:**
- A massive colonization ship designed to reach distant stars
- Carried Earth's best hope for humanity's survival
- Launched before the full collapse of civilization
- Intended destination: Habitable exoplanets for colonization

### The Blight (Unknown Date, ~2120s)

**The Catastrophe:**
- A world-ending event known as "The Blight" struck Earth
- Cities reduced to ruins, overtaken by wasteland sands
- "The green of corruption" spread across the remains of civilization
- A nuclear-devastated world left behind
- The few survivors struggled in the ruins

### The Long Sleep (Hundreds of Years)

**The Malfunction:**
- The Dregorian Voyager's hyperdrive mainframe malfunctioned
- Passengers remained in stasis for hundreds of years
- The ship drifted through space, unable to complete its mission
- Systems eventually brought the ship back toward Earth

### The Return (Present Day - Dregora Timeline)

**Homecoming to a Changed World:**
- The Dregorian Voyager returned to Earth after centuries
- The world was unrecognizable - transformed by the Blight
- Passengers awoke to a post-apocalyptic, mutated Earth
- The descendants of these colonists are the **Dregorians**
- This new world is what they call **Dregora**

### Current Era - The Age of Survival

**The World Today:**
- Ruins of past empires litter the landscape
- Ancient conflicts between lost civilizations left their mark
- Eldritch horrors and primal forces shape the land
- Humanity's descendants struggle to survive and rebuild
- Over 100 lore-focused books scattered throughout the world tell individual stories
- Libraries contain secrets, magical artifacts, and warnings of dangers to come

## Major Locations & Kingdoms

### The Kingdom of Daeroc
- One of the major surviving kingdoms
- Details found in lore books throughout the world
- Represents one of the post-return civilizations

### The City of Origins
- Significant settlement in Dregora
- Likely connected to the Dregorian colonists' first settlements
- A hub for trade and civilization

### Underground Cities
- Found in The Underneath dimension
- Ancient subterranean civilizations
- Connected to the surface via mysterious portals
- Very few explorers have discovered their full extent

### Medieval Cities
- Scattered throughout the overworld
- Built from the ruins of old world structures
- Mix of pre-Blight architecture and post-return construction
- Often contain valuable lore books and treasure

### Nuclear Ruins
- Remnants of pre-Blight civilization
- Dangerous due to lingering radiation and mutants
- Contains advanced technology and resources
- Often guarded by corrupted creatures

### Quarries
- Industrial remnants from the old world
- Source of valuable minerals and materials
- Often inhabited by dangerous creatures

### The Missile Silo Shelter
- Converted into a fallout shelter
- Now home to Gem Traders
- Represents the survival ingenuity of early post-Blight survivors

## Biome Groups & Regions

### Lush Regions
**Characteristics:** Areas where nature has reclaimed the land, often with mutated or magical properties

- **Ancient Rainforest** - Primordial jungle filled with ancient secrets
- **Corrupted Dense Jungle** - Twisted by the Blight's influence
- **Ravenous Jungle** - Aggressive, carnivorous flora
- **Carnivorous Forest** - Trees and plants that actively hunt
- **Mystic Grove** - Magical energies infuse the vegetation
- **Mangrove** - Coastal wetlands with unique ecology
- **Lavender Fields** - Peaceful areas of natural beauty (rare)
- **Cherry Blossom Grove** - Serene remnants of old world beauty

**Storytelling Potential:**
- Natural beauty contrasted with danger
- Hidden civilizations adapting to mutated nature
- Ancient guardians protecting sacred groves
- Herbalists and druids seeking natural magic

### Wasteland Regions
**Characteristics:** The Blight's strongest influence, nuclear devastation, hostile environments

- **Nuclear Ruins** - Irradiated cities of the old world
- **Dead Crimson Peaks** - Lifeless mountains stained red
- **Wastelands Plateau** - Elevated barren expanses
- **Wasteland Mountain** - Harsh, unforgiving terrain
- **Xeric Shrubland** - Dry, sparse vegetation
- **Dead Forest** - Petrified remains of ancient woods
- **Nuclear Swamp** - Poisoned wetlands
- **Salt Plains** - Crystallized, lifeless flats

**Storytelling Potential:**
- Survivors eking out existence in harsh conditions
- Mutant encounters
- Scavengers searching for old world technology
- Radiation sickness and corruption themes
- Desperate communities clinging to life

### Desert Regions
**Characteristics:** Vast expanses where water is scarce, extreme temperatures

- **Dead Wastelands** - Beyond hope of recovery
- **Traverse Mountainous Desert** - Rocky, difficult terrain
- **Traverse Badlands** - Eroded, maze-like canyons
- **Dunes** - Endless sand seas
- **Waste Mountain** - Desolate peaks

**Storytelling Potential:**
- Nomadic traders and wanderers
- Buried secrets beneath the sands
- Oasis settlements guarded jealously
- Sandstorm encounters
- Ancient ruins half-buried

### Fromimage Biomes
**Characteristics:** Static, pre-determined regions that exist across all Dregora worlds

- Consistent geography for important lore locations
- View these areas at https://map.dregora.com
- Major story locations are often Fromimage biomes

## Dimensions

### The Overworld (Dregora)
**Description:** The primary dimension, Earth transformed by the Blight

**Features:**
- 800+ distinct biomes (including parent, sub, and border biomes)
- 20,000+ biome objects (structures, trees, rocks, caves)
- Medieval cities, futuristic ruins, underground dungeons
- Ships (sunken, intact, stranded)
- Dynamic weather and environmental hazards

**Themes:**
- Post-apocalyptic survival
- Exploration and discovery
- Ancient mysteries
- Nature's reclamation vs. humanity's persistence

### The Underneath
**Description:** A subterranean dimension beneath Dregora

**Features:**
- Filled with parasitic creatures
- Ancient underground cities
- Mysterious portals connecting to surface
- Difficult to access from surface streets of underground cities

**Themes:**
- Body horror (parasites)
- Lost civilizations
- Darkness and claustrophobia
- Forbidden knowledge

**Lore Speculation:**
- Possibly a refuge during the Blight
- May be older than the surface civilizations
- Connection to eldritch forces mentioned in lore

### The Lost Cities (Dimension)
**Description:** A dimension of endless ruined urban sprawl

**Features:**
- Abandoned city buildings stretching infinitely
- Infested with parasites
- Valuable loot and resources
- Extremely dangerous

**Themes:**
- Urban decay
- Humanity's hubris
- Resource scarcity driving risk-taking
- Overwhelming odds

**Lore Connection:**
- May be a parallel dimension or alternate timeline
- Represents what Earth might have become without the Dregorians' return

## Factions & Civilizations

### The Dregorians (Player's Heritage)
**Identity:** Descendants of the Dregorian Voyager colonists

**Characteristics:**
- Resourceful and adaptable
- Mix of old world knowledge and new world survival skills
- Rebuilding civilization in a hostile world
- Value exploration and discovery

**Relationship to Others:** Varies - some accept them, others see them as outsiders

### Pre-Blight Survivors
**Identity:** Descendants of those who survived the Blight on Earth

**Characteristics:**
- Deep distrust of technology (it failed them before)
- Adapted to wasteland life over generations
- May see Dregorians as privileged "sleepers" who avoided the suffering
- Possess knowledge of the old world's collapse

**Potential Conflicts:** Resource competition, ideological differences, old grudges

### Mutants & Corrupted
**Identity:** Humans (or creatures) twisted by the Blight

**Characteristics:**
- Varying levels of intelligence and hostility
- Some may retain fragments of humanity
- Often violent and territorial
- Driven by primal needs or corrupt influences

**Storytelling Potential:** Tragic encounters, lost humanity, redemption arcs

### Eldritch Cults
**Identity:** Groups worshipping or harnessing primal forces

**Characteristics:**
- Seek power from the entities that shaped the Blight
- Perform dark rituals
- May have genuine supernatural abilities
- Range from desperate survivors to true believers

**Storytelling Potential:** Mystery, horror, moral ambiguity

### Nomadic Traders
**Identity:** Wandering merchants connecting settlements

**Characteristics:**
- Neutral parties in most conflicts
- Value information and rare goods
- Know secret routes and safe havens
- Often have colorful backgrounds

**Types:**
- **Gem Traders** - Deal in precious stones and crystals (found in 4 structure types)
- **Herbalists** - Sell plants, potions, and natural remedies (found in flowery plains)
- **General Merchants** - Offer varied goods and services

### The Guardians
**Identity:** Protectors of specific locations or artifacts

**Characteristics:**
- May be remnants of old world security
- Could be magical constructs
- Protect libraries, vaults, and sacred sites
- Often hostile to intruders but may be reasoned with

**Examples:**
- Brutal Tower bosses (end-game)
- Library guardians
- Tomb sentinels

## Key Structures & Points of Interest

### Gem Trader Structures (4 Variants)

1. **Old Missile Silo Fallout Shelter**
   - Converted military installation
   - Safe from surface dangers
   - Represents early survival ingenuity

2. **Swamp Trading Post**
   - Built on stilts or floating platforms
   - Adapted to wetland environment
   - Often foggy and mysterious atmosphere

3. **Small Tower Castle (Cold Regions)**
   - Medieval fortification
   - Built for defense against harsh winters and creatures
   - Stone construction, defensible position

4. **Modern White Building**
   - Pre-Blight architecture
   - Represents old world commerce
   - Often in urban ruins

### Herbalist Locations
- Found in flowery plain biomes
- Simple structures blending with nature
- Gardens of medicinal and magical plants

### Brutal Towers
- End-game content
- Heavily defended by powerful bosses
- Drops: Brutal coins (valuable currency)
- Represents major challenges and rewards

### Libraries
- Scattered throughout the world
- Contain lore books with world secrets
- May reveal hidden passages
- Guarded by magical or mechanical defenses
- Source of knowledge on "shadows yet to come"

### Dungeons
- Massive underground complexes
- Multiple levels and challenges
- Valuable treasure and artifacts
- Often themed (military bunker, ancient tomb, corrupted temple)

### Villages
- Small settlements of survivors
- Vary by biome and culture
- May request help or offer trade
- Can be protected or raided

### Ships (3 Types)
1. **Sunken Ships** - Underwater wrecks
2. **Intact Ships** - Functional or repairable vessels
3. **Stranded Ships** - Beached far from water (evidence of catastrophic events)

## Themes & Narrative Tone

### Primary Themes

**1. Survival Against the Odds**
- Scarcity of resources
- Hostile environment
- Constant danger from creatures and elements
- Community vs. isolation

**2. Lost Civilization & Rediscovery**
- Ruins hold secrets and technology
- Knowledge from before the Blight is precious
- Archaeological exploration
- Understanding the past to shape the future

**3. Nature's Reclamation**
- The world healing from human damage
- Plants and creatures evolved in strange ways
- Balance between preservation and progress
- Beauty in the ruins

**4. Corruption & Purity**
- The Blight's lingering influence
- Physical and moral corruption
- Struggles to remain "human" or "pure"
- Gray morality in desperate times

**5. Hope & Despair**
- Small victories matter
- Communities rebuilding
- But the world is harsh and unforgiving
- Success is fragile, failure is permanent

**6. Eldritch Mystery**
- Cosmic horror elements
- Forces beyond understanding
- Ancient entities and primal powers
- Knowledge that costs sanity

### Narrative Tone Guidelines

**Grimdark Fantasy with Hope:**
- The world is brutal and unforgiving
- Death is common, tragedy expected
- BUT: Small moments of humanity, beauty, and kindness stand out
- Heroes are flawed survivors, not paragons

**Post-Apocalyptic Exploration:**
- Sense of wonder mixed with danger
- Discovery of the old world's marvels and mistakes
- Environmental storytelling through ruins
- Mystery of what happened and what's to come

**Dark Fairy Tale:**
- Enchanted forests hide carnivorous plants
- Beautiful glowing mushrooms are toxic
- Mysterious benefactors have hidden costs
- Nothing is quite what it seems

**Lovecraftian Undertones:**
- Some mysteries are better left unsolved
- Ancient forces predate humanity
- Sanity is fragile
- Knowledge comes with a price

## Integration Guidelines for AI

### For Encounter Generation

**1. Biome-Appropriate Encounters**
- **Lush biomes:** Nature spirits, corrupted animals, herbalist camps, hidden groves
- **Wastelands:** Mutants, scavengers, radiation zones, desperate survivors
- **Deserts:** Nomads, sandstorms, buried ruins, dehydration challenges
- **Underground:** Parasites, lost miners, ancient technology, claustrophobia

**2. Lore-Consistent NPCs**
- **Dregorian:** Tech-savvy, optimistic, exploring heritage
- **Pre-Blight Survivor:** Wary, practical, distrustful of tech
- **Mutant:** Tragic, hostile, or neutral depending on corruption level
- **Trader:** Neutral, pragmatic, well-informed
- **Cultist:** Mysterious, potentially helpful or dangerous

**3. Structure-Based Encounters**
- Near **Libraries:** Scholars, guardians, lore reveals
- Near **Ruins:** Scavengers, ghosts of the past, dangerous tech
- Near **Villages:** Community quests, defense missions, social conflicts
- Near **Dungeons:** Adventurers, treasure hunters, dungeon delvers

**4. Thematic Consistency**
- Reference the Blight when appropriate
- Mention the Dregorian heritage for context
- Acknowledge the harsh survival environment
- Include moral ambiguity (no pure good/evil)
- Respect the grimdark tone while allowing hope

**5. Faction Reputation**
Suggested factions for the story system:
- **Dregorian Colonists** - Player's origin faction
- **Wasteland Nomads** - Trader and wanderer network
- **Daeroc Kingdom** - Established authority
- **Scavenger Guilds** - Ruin explorers and looters
- **Eldritch Cults** - Mysterious and dangerous
- **Nature's Guardians** - Druids, herbalists, protectors
- **The Corrupted** - Mutants and Blight-touched
- **Underground Cities** - Subterranean civilizations
- **Merchant League** - Gem traders and herbalists

**6. Story Thread Examples**

**Thread: "The Lost Voyager Crew"**
- Discover another cryo-pod from the Dregorian Voyager
- Survivor has different perspective on what to do in Dregora
- Leads to moral choices about technology and civilization

**Thread: "The Spreading Corruption"**
- Blight is advancing in a region
- Must find source or evacuate villagers
- Uncovers ancient Blight origins

**Thread: "The Underneath Beckons"**
- Strange dreams about underground city
- Portal discovered nearby
- Something wants the player to descend

**Thread: "The Last Library"**
- Rumors of a complete archive of old world knowledge
- Multiple factions seeking it
- Race to discover and protect/control it

**Thread: "The Brutal Tower Champion"**
- Training to challenge a tower boss
- Gathering legendary equipment
- Earning reputation with local warriors

**7. Environmental Storytelling**
- Reference nearby structures in encounters
- Use weather and time of day thematically
- Mention visible landmarks
- Connect to biome's unique characteristics

**8. Escalation Guidelines**
- **Early Game:** Small-scale survival, learning the world, meeting NPCs
- **Mid Game:** Faction conflicts, dungeon exploration, uncovering secrets
- **Late Game:** Dimension travel, major faction impacts, world-changing choices
- **End Game:** Brutal towers, ancient entities, fate of Dregora

**9. Book & Lore Integration**
- Encounters can reward lore books as treasure
- NPCs can reference book contents
- Quests to find specific books in libraries
- Books can unlock story threads or provide hints

**10. Tone Calibration**
- **Epic Mode:** Heroic struggles, grand narratives, hope prevails
- **Horror Mode:** Survival horror, psychological terror, Lovecraftian dread
- **Grimdark Mode:** Brutal reality, moral ambiguity, pyrrhic victories
- **Whimsical Mode:** Dark fairy tale, strange beauty, surreal encounters

## Appendix: Quick Reference

### Common Entity Types in Dregora
- **Mutants:** Humanoid corrupted by radiation/Blight
- **Parasites:** Infesting creatures from The Underneath
- **Bandits:** Hostile survivors preying on others
- **Wildlife:** Mutated animals (often aggressive)
- **Undead:** Raised by corruption or dark magic
- **Constructs:** Guardian golems and automatons
- **Eldritch Entities:** Unknowable horrors

### Common Items in Dregora
- **Gems:** Valuable currency and crafting materials
- **Herbs:** Medicinal and magical plants
- **Old World Tech:** Pre-Blight devices and weapons
- **Lore Books:** Knowledge of the world
- **Brutal Coins:** End-game currency
- **Radiation Medicine:** Essential for wasteland exploration
- **Purification Items:** Remove corruption/Blight effects

### Time Periods for Reference
- **2116:** Energy crisis and collapse
- **~2120s:** The Blight event
- **Unknown Years:** The Long Sleep
- **Present Day:** Centuries after the Blight, Dregorians have returned

### Key Questions for AI Context
1. What biome is the player in? (Affects encounter type)
2. What's the player's power level? (Affects difficulty)
3. Which factions has the player interacted with?
4. Has the player found lore books? (Affects knowledge)
5. Is the player near major structures?
6. What's the current narrative tone preference?
